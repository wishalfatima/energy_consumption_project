Week 7: Forecasting Evaluation & Improvement Attempt
This week I focused on improving the forecasting results. For this I tried different models. XG boost and Light GBM Recursive model.
Improvement Attempt — XGBoost
An alternative model using XGBoost was tested with an expanded feature set, including rolling statistics and cyclical seasonal features. Due to computational constraints, the model was trained on a reduced subset of the data.
This approach resulted in worse performance than the baseline. The primary issue was the reduction in training data, which negatively impacted model learning. This highlighted the importance of maintaining sufficient data when working with large-scale time series problems.
Improvement attempt Recursive Forecasting
After experimenting XG-Boost and discussion with my teammates I decided to apply Light GBM again but with recursive approach. In this approach each prediction is fed back into the model as input for future predictions. The feature set was extended to include additional lag and rolling statistics, providing a richer representation of recent consumption history. A 60-day historical window from the end of 2023 was used to initialize the forecasting process, ensuring sufficient data for lag and rolling features.
Implementation and Execution
A recursive forecasting function was implemented to generate predictions sequentially for each household. For every day in 2024:
Features were constructed from historical data and calendar variables
A prediction was generated and constrained to non-negative values
The prediction was added to the history for subsequent steps
This process was applied to all households, enabling full-year forecasts.
