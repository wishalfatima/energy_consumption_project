Week 1: Project setup & data exploration

The first week I focused mainly on building the theoretical foundation and setting up the project environment. The main objective was to understand the problem, review relevant literature, and prepare a structured workflow before starting implementation. I downloaded the data set and performed initial checks. We also made our group, got registered on Moodle and decided how would we work on this project.
Understanding the Problem & Setup

I started by carefully reading the assignment tasks and requirements. The project involved two main components:

1. Clustering households based on energy consumption behavior
2. Forecasting future energy consumption

At first, I was confused about how clustering and forecasting relate to each other.  The main challenge for me was understanding how clustering helps forecasting.
I researched and understood that clustering groups similar time series, allowing models to learn better patterns.

I studied what time series data is, concepts like trend, seasonality, and patterns. I realized that order of data matters, past values influence future values.

A key constraint identified early was the strict separation of training and evaluation data. The dataset structure and evaluation metric (average MAE) were also clearly understood.
I tried to research  different time series clustering approaches. I found out that Feature-based clustering as a more scalable approach. Key statistical features such as mean, standard deviation, coefficient of variation, and percentiles were identified as useful representations.
I created a project folder to organize all the tasks and files. I developed the environment to configured with all required libraries, including Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn. I downloaded the dataset and verified to ensure correct format and structure.
Data Loading and Verification
Both the 2023 and 2024 datasets were loaded using Pandas, with household IDs set as the index. Initial checks confirmed that the datasets were structurally consistent and aligned.
2023 data: 17,547 households with 365 daily readings
2024 data: 17,547 households with 366 daily readings (leap year)
Household IDs were consistent across both datasets

These checks ensured the dataset was reliable for further analysis.
